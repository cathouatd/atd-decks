The Real Problem

It was never
about the dogs.
It was the missing infrastructure.

Responsible dog owners follow rules when they have the tools to do so. Most people don't skip cleanup out of carelessness. They skip it because there are no bags, no bins, and no obvious place to go.

What was happening

  • Assumed owners bring their own bags. Most forget.
  • No designated relief areas. Dogs go wherever.
  • No signage reinforcing expectations.
  • No visible consequences, no social nudge.
  • Mall staff handle cleanup reactively.
  • Ban the dogs when it gets bad enough.

What we build instead

  • ATD x Earth Rated stations at every entrance. Bags, wipes, and pads always stocked.
  • Designated relief zones with clear signage and turf pads.
  • Behavioral nudge: "Most dog owners here clean up. Be one of them."
  • The Royalmount Dog Code. A community standard, not a rule.
  • ATD Welcome Zone as a certified anchor for the program.
  • Dogs welcome again. Problem solved at the source.

"People need concrete steps and protocols to follow. Cleaning up after a dog in public is still not second nature for most people, and that is not their fault."

The Royalmount Dog Code

Five simple steps.
Backed by behavioral science.

Behavior changes when expectations are clear, tools are available, and the community reinforces the norm.

1
📋

Acknowledge the Code

Scan the QR or grab a card at the entrance. Ten seconds. Sets the expectation before you walk in.

2
🧻

Grab Your Bags

Free bags and wipes at every ATD x Earth Rated station. No one walks in unprepared.

3
🐕

Use the Relief Zone

Designated outdoor areas with turf pads before entering. The right behavior, made easy.

4
✅

Clean Up. Always.

Bins everywhere. Social signage makes cleanup a community norm, not a chore.

5
🌟

Be an Ambassador

The owners who use the program become the program. The community owns the standard.

Why this works: Most people don't break norms on purpose. They break them because doing the right thing takes too much effort. Lower the friction (free bags, visible bins, clear zones), add social reinforcement ("most dog owners here clean up"), and compliance approaches near-100%. This is the same model that transformed public smoking behavior in a single generation.
