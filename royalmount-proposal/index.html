Gap

Recommended architecture

The right split is clear

Shopify should own

  • Storefront and checkout
  • Product and package commerce
  • Order records and customer commerce identity
  • Email marketing and campaign ecosystem
  • Subscription and commerce flows

PET CMS should own

  • Reservations and schedule operations
  • Pet profiles, care notes, compliance, daily logs
  • Boarding, daycare, grooming workflow state
  • Staff operations and service execution
  • Internal reporting and operations analytics

Critical missing layer

The strategy only becomes real when a proper integration layer exists for customer sync, package purchase mapping, entitlement redemption, invoice/payment signal sync, and source-of-truth rules between Shopify and PET CMS.

Build order

What Dan should build next, in exact order

Tier 1

  • Shopify integration layer
  • Grooming production workflow
  • Customer account depth + history correctness
  • Finance depth: payments, credits, deposits, package redemption

Tier 2

Do not keep adding pages. Harden the core loops until operators can trust the system without workarounds.

Fastest path to conviction

Five things that would change the verdict fast

1. Real Shopify sync

Purchase creates or updates the right customer and entitlement in PET CMS.

2. Grooming end-to-end

Booking to check-in to completion runs cleanly with invoice linkage and staff workflow.

3. Package trust

Balances and redemption are accurate, visible, and operationally reliable.

4. History integrity

Customer history reconciles correctly across reservations, invoices, and communications.

5. Messaging live

Real reminders and comms flow from actual workflows, not just stubs.
