The Real Problem

The dogs were never
the problem.

Irresponsible owners were. But even they only misbehave when there is no infrastructure to guide them. No bags, no bins, no designated areas, no clear expectations. People default to the path of least resistance. If that path includes walking past a mess, they will walk past it.

Before: No infrastructure

  • Assumed owners would bring their own bags. Most forgot.
  • No designated relief areas. Dogs went wherever.
  • No signage. No expectations set at the door.
  • No social nudge. No visible community standard.
  • Cleanup was reactive. Staff chased problems.
  • When it got bad enough, ban the dogs.

After: Built for success

  • ATD x Earth Rated stations at every entrance. Bags, wipes, pads stocked.
  • Designated relief zones with turf, drainage, and clear signage.
  • The Dog Code sets expectations before anyone walks in.
  • Social norm signage: "Most dog owners here clean up. Join them."
  • ATD Welcome Zone as the flagship anchor.
  • Controlled reintroduction, with the risk contained at the root.

"Give people the tools. They will do the right thing."

The Royalmount Dog Code

Five steps.
Simple, visible, enforceable.

The Dog Code is not the program. It is the behavioral layer that supports the program, clarifies expectations, and reduces avoidable incidents.

1
📋

Read the Code

Scan the QR or grab a card at the entrance. Ten seconds. Sets the tone before you walk in.

2
🧻

Grab Your Bags

Free bags and wipes at every ATD x Earth Rated station. No one enters unprepared.

3
🐕

Use the Relief Zone

Designated outdoor areas with turf pads before entering. The right behavior, made effortless.

4
✅

Clean Up. Always.

Bins at every station. Social signage turns cleanup into a shared standard, not a chore.

5
🌟

Be the Standard

Owners who use the program become the program. The community polices itself.

Why this works: People are more likely to comply when the expected behavior is visible, easy, and reinforced. Free bags, visible bins, designated zones, clear signage, and a defined response path reduce friction and improve consistency without creating heavy enforcement burden.
